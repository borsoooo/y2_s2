FIBA (Fédération Internationale de Basketball) is the international governing body for the sport of basketball, founded in 1932 and headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland.

It establishes the official rules of play, sets equipment standards, and oversees international competitions—including the FIBA Basketball World Cup and various continental championships (such as FIBA Europe’s events). In addition to organizing tournaments, FIBA promotes the global development of basketball by supporting youth programs, coaching education, and infrastructure growth in markets around the world. Through its work with national federations, FIBA aims to make basketball accessible, competitive, and culturally engaging on every continent.

BIP Intercultural Marketing Project – Turkish Farmers

Conducted in-depth research on Turkish farmers for Solis Tractor/Yanmar as part of an intercultural marketing competition in Turkey

Client: Solis/ Yanmar Turkey

Learn More

Project Highlights:

  • As part of the 2025 BIP Intercultural Marketing and Project Management course, I joined a team tasked by Solis Tractor (Yanmar) to analyze market entry strategies for Turkish farmers.
  • Conducted qualitative interviews and surveys with small-scale and large-scale farmers in multiple Turkish provinces
  • Analyzed cultural, economic, and logistical factors influencing tractor adoption and brand perception

Key Takeaways:

  • This project deepened my understanding of how to bridge cultural gaps in B2B marketing. It also taught me how to structure research, synthesize diverse data points, and propose actionable strategies in an international context.

The BIP (Blended Intensive Programme) is a short-term international program supported by the Erasmus+ framework that combines online learning with a physical mobility experience. It brings together students from different countries to collaborate on real-world projects, develop intercultural skills, and gain hands-on experience in a specific field—such as marketing, business, or sustainability.

Tales Behind the Wheels – Short Documentary

Explored the social perceptions, fears, and realities of hitchhiking through a creative documentary experiment

Client: Breda University of Applied Sciences (School Project)

Learn More

Project Highlights:

  • Tales Behind the Wheels is an experimental short documentary we created as part of an assignment. The film focuses on the cultural and psychological barriers surrounding hitchhiking in Europe, exploring why many people fear it and how those fears are shaped by media, personal stories, and assumptions.

Key Takeaways:

  • We conducted real-life hitchhiking experiments across the Netherlands to document reactions and social dynamics
  • Interviewed both drivers and hitchhikers to capture authentic perspectives
  • Handled all production: filming, editing, narration, and soundtrack design using Adobe Premiere Pro
  • Presented our findings through a mix of documentary-style footage and reflective voiceovers

This project taught me how to approach social topics creatively and responsibly, while sharpening my skills in media production, editing, and narrative structure. It also sparked interesting discussions about human trust, judgment, and the influence of fear on behavior.
